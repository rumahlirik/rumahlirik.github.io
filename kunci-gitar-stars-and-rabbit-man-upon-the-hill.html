Moral Value: Think before you do something. Consider the consequences, disadvantages, and advantages.

The Vulture, The Elephant, and The Rabbit

(Burung Bangkai, Gajah, dan Kelinci)

Pesan moral yang terdapat pada cerita di atas adalah tidak semua hal yang terdapat di mimpi kita harus dilakukan sama persis dengan yang ada di dalam mimpi. Terlebih lagi jika di dalam mimpi tersebut ada hal-hal yang dapat menyakiti makhluk lain. Kita harus bijaksana dalam mengambil setiap keputusan yang kita putuskan. Berpikir ulang sebelum memerintahkan sesuatu, jika memang posisi kita adalah sebagai pemimpin. Ada baiknya sebelum melakukan atau mengatakan sesuatu, tanyakan pada diri sendiri bagaimana jika ada orang lain yang berlaku demikian terhadap kita, akankah menyenangkan atau merugikan kita. Jika hal tersebut membuat kita senang, kita masih harus berpikir ulang, apakah semua orang dapat menganggapnya sebagai hal yang menyenangkan juga atau tidak. Karena semua orang tidaklah sama. Intinya, berpikir sebelum bertindak agar tidak merugikan diri sendiri ataupun pihak lain.

Sinopsis:

The Vulture, The Elephant, and The Rabbit

(Burung Bangkai, Gajah, dan Kelinci)

Cerita ini menceritakan tentang Raja Burung Bangkai yang bermimpi memakan daging gajah putih. Setelah ia bangun dari tidurnya, ia memerintahkan pelayan-pelayannya untuk mencarikannya gajah putih agar ia dapat memakan dagingnya.

Para pelayan pun patuh dan segera berangkat mencari gajah putih. Mereka pun menemukannya dan memintanya untuk ikut ke kerajaan bersama mereka. Sang gajah pun sedih namun ia tidak dapat berbuat banyak. Dia berpamitan dengan istri dan anaknya untuk pergi ke kerajaan menjadi santapan Raja Burung Bangkai.

Di dalam perjalanan, mereka bertemu seekor kelinci. Sang kelinci bertanya hendak kemanakah sang gajah. Sang gajah pun menceritakan semuanya. Lalu sang kelinci memutuskan untuk ikut.

Setelah sampai di kerajaan, sang Raja Burung Bangkai pun senang melihat para pelayannya datang bersama Gajah Putih yang dia inginkan. Namun, dia terkejut saat ingin memotong belalai sang gajah. Karena sang raja melihat seekor kelinci di atas kepala gajah.

Sang raja bertanya apa maksud kelinci tersebut berada di sana. Sang kelinci pun mengatakan bahwa dia bermimpi menikahi adik dari Sang Raja. Sang Raja pun terkejut dan marah atas apa yang dikatakan oleh kelinci. Raja sudah barang tentu tidak menyetujui adiknya menikah dengan seekor kelinci. Lalu kelinci pun mengatakan bahwa Sang Raja juga tidak semestinya melakukan apa yang ada di dalam mimpinya. Sang raja pun sadar bahwa apa yang dia hendak lakukan adalah salah. Sang gajah dan kelinci pun kembali ke dalam hutang dan bertemu dengan keluarga mereka.

Narrative Text

Cerita di atas merupakan Narrative Text. Narrative Text adalah teks yang berisi tentang cerita yang terjadi di masa lampau. Cerita tersebut dapat berupa fabel (cerita yang pemerannya hewan seperti cerita di atas), legenda (seperti cerita asal muasal terbentuknya sesuatu hal), atau fairy tale (kisah fiksi yang pemeran dan ceritanya tidak nyata, namun terdapat hikmah yang dapat dipetik dari kisah tersebut). Narrative Text dibuat untuk menghibur para pembacanya. Tense yang dipakai adalah Simple Past Tense. Karena cerita yang terdapat di dalam Narrative Text adalah cerita di masa lampau.

7. Compare the cat and rabbit aesophagus


Jawaban:

The esophagus, also known as the food pipe, is a muscular tube that connects the throat to the stomach. Both cats and rabbits have esophagi, but there are some differences between them.

In terms of anatomy, the cat's esophagus is relatively straight and has a uniform diameter along its length. On the other hand, the rabbit's esophagus has a more pronounced curve or bend, which allows them to perform hindgut fermentation. This adaptation helps rabbits digest fibrous plant material more efficiently.

Another difference is the size of the esophagus. Cats are larger animals compared to rabbits, so their esophagi are typically larger in diameter and length.

Additionally, the muscle contractions, or peristalsis, that propel food through the esophagus may differ between cats and rabbits, although they both exhibit this movement.

Overall, while both cats and rabbits have esophagi, there are variations in anatomy and adaptations to accommodate their different digestive systems and diets.

Object pronouns adalah kata ganti milik (orang atau benda) yang berfungsi sebagai “object”. Dengan kata lain “kata ganti milik yang dikenai tindakan”.

Contoh: I make a cake

Yang merupakan objek pada kalimat di di atas adalah "cake" karena "cake"-lah yang menjadi sasaran subjek untuk dibuat.

37. tolong buatkan naskah drama the white rabbit and the sharks


The White Rabbit Story

There once lived a white rabbit on a small island. One day, when he was on the beach, he saw a large island in the distance.

“There must be a lot of good fresh grass on that island. But how can I go to it without a boat?” while he was thinking about what to do a big shark swam close to the beach. The rabbit saw the shark and called to him. “Hello, Mr. Shark, you look a clever fellow. Do you think there are more sharks than rabbits in this world?”

“We would have to count them to find that out,” answered the shark.

“Well,” said the rabbit, “You line up your sharks between this island and that one and I’ll count them.”

“Very well,” said the shark, and he called to all of his friends to come and float side by side between the two islands. When they were ready, the rabbit jumped from one shark to the next counting them. “One, two, three …..” he had almost reached the other island when he laughed and said without thinking, “You stupid shark! What is the use of counting you? I only wanted to cross over to this island.”

At once, the sharks were very angry because they did not like being cheated. They began to bite the white rabbit who asked them to forgive him. Finally they let go but all of his lovely white fur was torn off. His skin was bare.

He sat down and cried. Soon three men came along and asked him why he was crying. The rabbit told them what had happened. The men looked at each other and then one of them said, “The best thing you can do is to wash yourself in the sea and then let the sun dry you. Your wounds will heal and your white fur will grow again.”

The rabbit ran back to the sea and then sat in the sun. But his skin was only more painful. It began to hurt so badly that he cried out, “Who will save my life? Help! Help!”

A young man, who was coming along the road, stopped when he heard the rabbit crying. “What has happened to you, Rabbit?”

He asked. He felt sorry for the rabbit. The poor creature could not stop crying and told his story as the tears ran down his face. The young man was annoyed when he heard how the rabbit had been treated. “The three men did not give you good advice. Please forgive them. I’ll help you. Now, go and wash your body in fresh river water and I’ll get a bed ready for you.”

The rabbit did as he was told and returned to rest on the soft grass bed which the young man had made ready for him. The poor rabbit rested and was warm and comfortable. His white fur grew again. He was so happy that he did not try to cheat anyone again.
